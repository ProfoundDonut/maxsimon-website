Welcome

Hey, welcome to my site! First, let me explain that this is not my first maxsimon.io site, nope, its my third! This is the third time, and hopefully last. As I am writing, this, I realize that the following statement was most liley false. So if you are reading this, than I guess I got lucky with this one. If you really want to see the crappy original website, go ahead and click here. Anyway, this site is mainly about me, a web developer, designer, 3D artist, photographer, VFX editor, etc (the list goes on). I first found a love for technology at a very young age, and have started coding. So as the frist "real" project, I created a basic website with some information about myself. Now that last statement was a lie, it just had a ton of lorem ipsum. And honestly, when people viseted, they probably thought I used a template and changed a couple names. About a year later, I still have not changed it, and my design skills have vastly impoved, so I decided to start a brand new site, a fresh start essentially. So, I am writing this: a simple introduction about myself.

So, coding, lets start with that. Well, obviously im not awful at programming, but I also have a pretty long way to doing anything life changing. A couple years ago, I learned HTML, pretty much the easiest language out there, next to like Visual BASIC or something. Anyway, the easiest language that is 20 years old and is actually relavent today. So I got pretty good at it. I started learning more and mroe advaced CSS, and got to the point where my websites looked pretty profesional. I was pretty excited about this new skill that only took me like 6 months to get okay at. So, what your thinking is correct, I started in web, and web is where my heart is. I am good, I have fun, and I personally love the fact that I can view this website anywhere, no installation or anything needed. Well, with the basics of web progamming under my belt, I set off on learning more complex stuff. I stearted off with PHP, cause' I thought it'd be fun to host my own sites, and be able to manage them. Well I was an idiot. Let me tell you a secret, NEVER LEARN PHP (or any server/database access language) BEFORE JS. So I set off learning worlds worst language and about a month later, I stopped, and started learning JavaScript. Which really helped me, like a lot. The thing is, JavaScript is pretty donting for most people, the syntax doesn't make sense, and with most tutorials, the stuff they have you starting off learning, is boring as crap. But, get through the boring stuff and you'll get to the interesting stuff soon enouph, JS is too helpful to ignore. After that, I tried programming complex stuff, like logins, and stuff that you really shouldnt try doing with JS. So I asked a good friend if there was like a C++ language for the web, and he refered me to "Ruby On Rails". So I still in the middle of learning that. Which honestly is super awesome. I feel like I actually am programming webapps, time will tell where that takes me, but I think that should be pretty good. Well, on to the next paragraph of me rambling on about myself.

Design really goes hand-in-hand with progamming. I learned that the hard way. It's a stupid idea to try to design stuff in CSS. So after about a year and a half of designing all my websites directly in CSS, I forked over the money for Affinity Designer. If you haven't heard of it, it's like a better, cheaper version of Adobe Illutstrator. I had some basic knowledge of Illustrator, and at the time I pretty good at Photoshop so I picked of Designed quickly. The problem was, I had only ever designed "tabletop" parallax websites, that I thought were cool at the time, but were actually kind of crap. I found that I, a person who understood how to use these super creative tools, was like the least creative person ever. So I thought. Somethiems creativity needs to be expressed in different ways for everyone alive. For me, design was a good way. I had never been great at creating anything that was truley "from scratch". Everything I did had been inspired, or coppied, or done by someone else with me. So when I started letting go, and letting my brain just do whatever feels natural, I started having a lot of fun. I loved that I could create something that had never been created before. Something that nobody else had ever scene, or thought of, or even came remotly close to dreaming of. It was something truley unique to me. After I started actually enjoying designing, I stayed up all night watching tutorial and other crap that eventually made me really good at graphic design. I had an eye for what went where, and what looked bad. I instantly saw everything in everything, which probably doesnt make sense to you. I saw design in everyday life, in normal objects, in everyhing. I started thinking about different ideas every day. I started really understanding the world, from a designers standpoint. Now this is probably me just thinking im better than I am, but to me, I have come so far in the design aspect since I first started web design.
